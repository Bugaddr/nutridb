myths:
  - myth: Biotin supplements make hair grow faster
    reality: Biotin supplements only help hair if you are deficient (rare). For people with normal biotin levels, supplements provide no hair growth benefit — a massive industry built on weak evidence.
  - myth: More biotin is always better
    reality: High-dose biotin supplements can interfere with immunoassay lab tests, causing falsely low troponin (masking heart attacks) or falsely abnormal thyroid results. The FDA issued a safety warning in 2017.
precautions:
  - STOP biotin supplements at least 72 hours before blood tests
  - Cooking egg whites denatures avidin, making the biotin available
  - Inform your doctor if taking biotin supplements before any lab tests
interactions:
  - substance: Raw egg whites
    effect: Avidin protein binds biotin, preventing absorption (12 raw eggs/day can cause deficiency)
  - substance: Anticonvulsants (carbamazepine, phenytoin)
    effect: Accelerate biotin catabolism
  - substance: Lab tests (troponin, TSH, T4)
    effect: High-dose biotin causes false results in streptavidin-biotin immunoassays
history: Discovered through the "egg white injury" phenomenon — animals fed raw egg whites developed dermatitis and hair loss. In 1936, Fritz Kögl and B. Tönnis isolated the factor and named it biotin (from Greek "bios" = life). The avidin-biotin interaction is now one of the strongest non-covalent bonds known and is widely used in laboratory research.
funFacts:
  - The biotin-streptavidin bond is one of the strongest in nature (Kd ~10⁻¹⁵ M) — used extensively in molecular biology
  - You'd need to eat about 12 raw egg whites daily to develop biotin deficiency
  - The biotin supplement industry is worth hundreds of millions despite limited evidence for its marketed benefit
  - Rocky's raw egg habit would eventually cause biotin deficiency if sustained
