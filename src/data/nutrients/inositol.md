myths:
  - myth: Inositol is a B vitamin
    reality: It was once called vitamin B8, but since the body can synthesize it from glucose, it doesn't meet the definition of a vitamin. The name persists in some supplement marketing.
precautions:
  - 40:1 ratio of myo-inositol to D-chiro-inositol is considered optimal for PCOS
  - Generally very safe — doses of 18g/day have been used in OCD studies
interactions:
  - substance: Metformin
    effect: Both improve insulin sensitivity — may have additive effects
  - substance: Lithium
    effect: Lithium reduces brain inositol levels — this may be part of its therapeutic mechanism
history: Inositol was discovered in 1850 by Joseph Scherer. Initially classified as vitamin B8, it was later reclassified when found to be synthesized endogenously. Its resurgence as a therapeutic compound for PCOS began in the early 2000s with Italian clinical trials.
funFacts:
  - Your kidneys produce about 4 grams of inositol per day from glucose
  - The 40:1 myo-inositol to D-chiro-inositol ratio mirrors the ratio found naturally in human ovarian tissue
  - Inositol exists in 9 different stereoisomers, but myo-inositol is the most abundant biological form
