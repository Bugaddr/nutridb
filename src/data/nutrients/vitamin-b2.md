description: Key to energy production and cellular function. Acts as a precursor to FAD and FMN, coenzymes in numerous redox reactions.
functions:
  - Precursor to FAD and FMN — essential electron carriers in metabolism
  - Required for fat, drug, and steroid metabolism
  - Maintains healthy skin, eyes, and nervous system
  - Helps convert B6 and folate to their active forms
  - Antioxidant role through glutathione reductase

myths:
  - myth: If your urine is yellow from B2, you're overdosing
    reality: Bright yellow urine from riboflavin is normal and harmless — it just means your body excreted what it didn't need. It's one of the safest vitamins.
precautions:
  - UV light destroys riboflavin — milk stored in clear containers loses significant B2
  - Oral contraceptives may slightly reduce riboflavin status
interactions:
  - substance: Tricyclic antidepressants
    effect: May reduce riboflavin absorption
  - substance: Phenobarbital
    effect: Increases riboflavin metabolism
history: Riboflavin was isolated from milk whey in 1933. Its yellow-green fluorescence was noted early — the name comes from "ribose" (sugar component) + "flavin" (Latin flavus = yellow). It was synthesized in 1935 by Richard Kuhn, who received the 1938 Nobel Prize.
funFacts:
  - Riboflavin is the reason your multivitamin turns your urine neon yellow
  - It's used as a food coloring (E101) — giving some cheeses and sauces their yellow color
  - High-dose riboflavin (400 mg/day) is an evidence-based migraine preventive
