---
name: Manganese
altName: Mn
symbol: Mn
category: mineral
subcategory: trace
essentiality: essential
sortOrder: 25
description: Trace mineral essential for bone formation, blood clotting, and metabolism. Cofactor for several important enzymes including MnSOD (mitochondrial antioxidant defense).
functions:
  - Cofactor for MnSOD (mitochondrial superoxide dismutase — key antioxidant)
  - Bone formation and cartilage synthesis
  - Amino acid, cholesterol, and carbohydrate metabolism
  - Blood clotting
  - Wound healing
rda:
  nih:
    - group: Adult males
      amount: 2.3 mg (AI)
    - group: Adult females
      amount: 1.8 mg (AI)
  who:
    - group: Adults
      amount: No specific recommendation (considered adequate in normal diets)
  efsa:
    - group: Adults
      amount: 3 mg (AI)
deficiency:
  symptoms:
    - Impaired growth, skeletal abnormalities
    - Altered carbohydrate and lipid metabolism
    - Skin rash
    - Extremely rare from diet alone
  riskGroups:
    - People with epilepsy (lower levels observed)
    - People with osteoporosis
toxicity:
  symptoms:
    - Manganism — Parkinson-like symptoms (from occupational inhalation)
    - Cognitive impairment
    - Tremors, gait disturbance
  upperLimit: 11 mg/day for adults
  notes: Toxicity is primarily an occupational hazard (miners, welders). Oral toxicity from food is rare except in people with liver disease (impaired excretion).
normalLevels:
  bloodRange: 4.7-18.3 ng/mL (whole blood)
  unit: ng/mL
testing:
  methods:
    - Whole blood manganese
    - MRI of the brain (manganese accumulates in basal ganglia — visible on T1-weighted imaging)
  sampleType: Blood (whole blood)

myths:
  - myth: Manganese and magnesium are the same
    reality: Despite similar names, they are completely different elements with different functions. Manganese (Mn) is a trace mineral needed in mg amounts; magnesium (Mg) is a major mineral needed in hundreds of mg.
precautions:
  - People with liver disease should limit manganese — they can't excrete excess
  - Welders and miners should use protective equipment
  - Infant soy formula contains more manganese than breast milk or cow's milk formula
interactions:
  - substance: Iron
    effect: Shares absorption pathways — high iron intake may reduce manganese absorption and vice versa
  - substance: Calcium and phosphorus
    effect: May slightly reduce manganese absorption
history: Manganese was isolated in 1774 by Johan Gottlieb Gahn. Its name comes from the Latin "magnes" (magnet). Manganism from mine exposure was first described in the 1800s. Its essentiality in humans wasn't confirmed until the 1970s.
funFacts:
  - Manganism (manganese toxicity) causes symptoms nearly identical to Parkinson's disease
  - Tea is a significant dietary source of manganese — 1 cup provides ~0.5 mg
  - Manganese dioxide was used in cave paintings 30,000 years ago as a black pigment
