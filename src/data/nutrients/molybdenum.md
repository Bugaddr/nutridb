history: Molybdenum was discovered as an element in 1778 by Carl Wilhelm Scheele. Its biological role was established in the 1950s when molybdenum cofactor (Moco) was identified in xanthine oxidase. The only documented human dietary deficiency was in a TPN patient in 1981.
funFacts:
  - Only ONE case of dietary molybdenum deficiency has ever been documented in a human
  - The word comes from Greek "molybdos" (lead) because it was confused with lead ore
  - Cattle are far more sensitive to molybdenum imbalance than humans — excess causes "teart" disease
