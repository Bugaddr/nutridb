name: Potassium
altName: K (from Latin Kalium)
symbol: K
category: mineral
subcategory: major
essentiality: essential
sortOrder: 17
description: Essential electrolyte for nerve transmission, muscle contraction, and fluid balance. Most people don't get enough — deficiency is one of the most common electrolyte disorders.
functions:
  - Maintains cellular fluid balance (sodium-potassium pump)
  - Essential for nerve impulse transmission
  - Muscle contraction (including heart)
  - Blood pressure regulation (counteracts sodium)
  - Kidney stone prevention

  who:
    - group: Adults

  efsa:
    - group: Adults
      amount: 3500 mg
deficiency:
  symptoms:
    - Muscle weakness and cramps
    - Fatigue
    - Constipation
    - Heart palpitations, arrhythmias
    - Tingling and numbness
  conditions:
    - Hypokalemia — can be life-threatening
    - Cardiac arrhythmias
    - Paralytic ileus (gut paralysis)
  riskGroups:
    - People on diuretics
    - Chronic diarrhea or vomiting
    - Excessive sweating (athletes)
    - Eating disorders
    - IBD patients
toxicity:
  symptoms:
    - Cardiac arrhythmias (potentially fatal)
    - Muscle weakness, paralysis
    - Nausea
  upperLimit: No established UL from food. Supplement doses should be ≤99 mg per tablet (FDA regulation)
  notes: Hyperkalemia is a medical emergency. Usually caused by kidney failure or potassium-sparing drugs, not diet.
normalLevels:
  bloodRange: 3.5-5.0 mEq/L
  unit: mEq/L
  notes: Serum potassium is tightly regulated. Even small deviations can be life-threatening.
testing:
  methods:
    - Serum potassium (basic metabolic panel)
    - 24-hour urine potassium
    - ECG changes (peaked T waves in hyperkalemia)
  sampleType: Blood (serum)

myths:
  - myth: Bananas are the best source of potassium
    reality: Potatoes have over 2x more potassium than bananas. White beans, sweet potatoes, and avocados also beat bananas.
precautions:
  - People with kidney disease must limit potassium — the kidneys can't excrete excess
  - Potassium supplements are limited to 99 mg per tablet to prevent cardiac events
  - Salt substitutes contain potassium chloride — dangerous for kidney patients
interactions:
  - substance: ACE inhibitors / ARBs
    effect: Can increase potassium levels — monitor closely
  - substance: Potassium-sparing diuretics (spironolactone)
    effect: Risk of dangerous hyperkalemia
  - substance: Loop/thiazide diuretics
    effect: Increase potassium excretion — may need supplementation
history: Humphry Davy isolated potassium in 1807 through electrolysis of potash — making it the first metal ever isolated by electrolysis. Its role in nerve and muscle function was understood through the work of Hodgkin and Huxley (Nobel Prize 1963) on the action potential.
funFacts:
  - Potassium (K) comes from "Kalium" (Latin) which comes from "al-qalyah" (Arabic for plant ash)
  - Your body contains about 140g of potassium — more than sodium
  - Potassium-40 in your body makes you slightly radioactive — about 4,400 nuclear decays per second
  - Only 2% of Americans meet the adequate intake for potassium
